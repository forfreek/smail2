## Online Temporary Email

Use an online temporary inbox when you need to receive email in the browser right away without setting up a mail app, account, or separate client.

This is useful when the task is immediate and short-lived: open the page, copy the address, wait for the message, and move on.

### When browser access is the right fit

- Immediate access without installing apps
- Quick inbox refresh for live verification flows
- Better separation between disposable and personal addresses

These are situations where speed matters more than long-term mailbox ownership.

### How to use it well

- Copy the address exactly to avoid delivery errors.
- If the email does not appear, resend from the source platform.
- Wait a short moment and refresh again because sender queues can delay delivery.

### What this workflow is good for

- OTP codes
- verification links
- activation messages
- one-off signup checks

### What it does not replace

An online inbox is still a temporary inbox. It does not replace a personal mailbox for:

- long-term account ownership
- account recovery
- private communication
- work or financial accounts

### Retention and boundary

Messages are kept for up to 24 hours and then removed automatically. Save anything important before the window ends.

### Decision rule

Use the online inbox when you need immediate browser access to a short-lived message.

Do not use it when the email address needs to remain part of a long-term account relationship.
