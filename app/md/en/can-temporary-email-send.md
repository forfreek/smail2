## Can Temporary Email Send Messages?

In most cases, no. Temporary inboxes are usually built for receiving messages, not for sending them back out.

That is not an accident. It is one of the boundaries that keeps this kind of inbox usable for quick verification workflows.

### Why receive-only is common

- Lower abuse risk (spam, fraud, phishing)
- Simpler anti-abuse and moderation controls
- Faster infrastructure for receive-only inboxes

These constraints are part of why temporary inboxes can stay simpler and faster.

### What this means for trashmail users

You should treat trashmail as a place to receive:

- OTP codes
- signup confirmations
- activation links
- verification messages you only need once

You should not assume the address can later be used for:

- sending replies
- account conversations
- support threads
- ongoing communication

### When to use a permanent mailbox instead

Use a long-term provider if you need:

- Sending and replying to messages
- Account ownership and identity continuity
- Long-term archives and recovery workflows

### Decision rule

Use trashmail for receiving messages you only need briefly.

Use a permanent mailbox whenever sending is part of the workflow.
