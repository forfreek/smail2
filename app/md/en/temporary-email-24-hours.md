## 24 Hour Temporary Email

Use a 24-hour inbox when a verification or signup flow may not finish in one short session. That is where a longer retention window matters.

The point is not just to keep email longer. The point is to give enough time for delayed messages, follow-up OTP requests, or registration steps that do not complete immediately.

### When a 24-hour inbox is useful

- Confirmation links arrive late
- You must complete registration across multiple sessions
- A second OTP is sent hours after the first step

These are the cases where a 10-minute mailbox can expire before the workflow does.

### How to use it

1. Generate a temporary address.
2. Use it on the target website or app.
3. Open your inbox on trashmail.space.
4. Read messages and copy required details.

Messages are removed after the 24-hour window ends, so anything you still need should be saved before that point.

### What the longer window does not change

A 24-hour inbox is still a temporary inbox. It does not become a permanent mailbox just because it lasts longer.

Do not use it for:

- banking or payment accounts
- work, school, legal, or government systems
- long-term account recovery

### Best practice

- Use it for low-risk workflows only
- Save needed details before the window ends
- Move important accounts to a permanent mailbox later

### Decision rule

Use the 24-hour inbox when the workflow may stretch across delays, retries, or multiple sessions.

Do not use it when the account itself needs durable email ownership.
